Astronomy 310 — Stellar Astrophysics

Intermediate

An intermediate course on the properties, structure, and evolution of stars. The course develops the physics of stars in the order an astronomer encounters them: the observed properties of stars and the Hertzsprung–Russell diagram; stellar atmospheres and radiative transfer; the equations of stellar structure, including the equation of state, energy transport, and nuclear energy generation; and finally stellar evolution — the main sequence, post-main-sequence phases, and stellar remnants such as white dwarfs, neutron stars, and black holes.

Astronomy 104 — Our Exploration of the Solar System

Introductory

An introductory survey of the Solar System for non-majors. The course examines the Sun, the terrestrial and giant planets and their moons, and the small bodies — asteroids, comets, and dwarf planets — together with the formation and evolution of the planetary system, the comparative geology and atmospheres of the planets, the history of Solar System exploration, and the search for habitable environments and life beyond Earth. Emphasis is placed on the physical principles that shape planetary worlds and on how we come to know them through observation and spacecraft missions.
