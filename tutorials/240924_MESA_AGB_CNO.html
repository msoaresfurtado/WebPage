We want to explore the star at its largest size, which is the during the thermally pulsating asymptotic giant branch, also known as the TPAGB.

We will investigate surface abundances using two files. File one (f1) will be a low-metallicity ([Feh]=-0.1) MIST model of a 2.0 Msun star obtained here: https://waps.cfa.harvard.edu/MIST/interp_tracks.html

File two (f2) is one of the many AGB stellar tracks ran by Meridith Joyce, which can be found here: https://zenodo.org/records/11282597

Some notes: The tar.gz archive files labeled with a string of the form "AGB_to_end_M1.XX_y-fixed.tar.gz" include all models having a mass beginning with 1 (1.00, 1.10, 1.20, etc) and adopting the fixed helium assumption.

Anything that starts with "history" and ends with ".data" is an evolutionary track. Tracks that have "_p3" are the third phase: AGB to end (or failure of the model) and these files are large.

In the file name, the two-decimal number after m, four-decimal number after z and 3-decimal number after y encode the evolutionary track's initial mass, initial metallicity (Z) and initial helium abundance (Y), respectively. In cases where there is no y value provided, the helium abundance is fixed as described in the paper.

f2 will be a 2.0 Msun star with z0.0100 (close to [Fe/H]=-0.1) and a Y=0.01.
